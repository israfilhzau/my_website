About Me

I am Israfil Hossen, a passionate and dedicated researcher with a strong academic foundation and a focus on advancing sustainable agricultural practices. I hold a PhD in Plant Nutrition (Soil Science) from the University of China Academy of Science (UCAS) China, MS in Food Science and Technology (Microbiology), and a Bachelor of Science in Agriculture. My research centers on the intricate relationships between soil health, plant nutrition, and microbial interactions, aiming to develop innovative solutions to enhance crop productivity and promote food security on a global scale.

With a deep interest in bioinformatics and molecular biology, I utilize cutting-edge technologies to investigate genomic, transcriptomic, and microbial data, driving insights that inform more sustainable agricultural systems. By combining my interdisciplinary expertise with rigorous experimentation, I strive to contribute valuable knowledge that can transform agricultural practices and improve global food production, all while minimizing environmental impact.
